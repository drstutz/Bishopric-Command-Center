Leadership Principles — 2nd Counselor
1
Serve the Bishop's Vision, Not Your Own
Your role is to sustain and extend the Bishop's vision, not to run a parallel agenda. Bring ideas through proper channels. Disagree privately, unify publicly.
2
Know the People in Your Stewardship
Names, faces, situations. Primary children. Deacons. Investigators in the pipeline. A leader who doesn't know his people can't serve them. Presence precedes influence.
3
Courage Over Comfort
Step out of the boat. Hard conversations, difficult interviews, and showing up for the ward's outliers — that's the work. Comfort is the anti-value.
4
Own Your Assignments Completely
Be the reason things get done. Don't wait for the Bishop to follow up on your stewardships. Ownership means no dropped balls, no excuses. Find a way.
5
Arrows Out — The Ward First
Ego has no place here. Every meeting, every decision — arrows pointing outward toward the members you serve. Service over self-promotion.
6
Guard Your Family While You Serve
Pierina, Ian, Luca, Leo, and Andrew come before the ward. A calling never justifies a neglected covenant. Schedule your family before you schedule the ward.
